## Organizational Capacity

Midland Youth Services (MYS) has operated in the greater Midland region for 22 years,
delivering evidence-based mental health and after-school programming to underserved youth
ages 10–18. Since 2015, MYS has administered $14.2 million in federal, state, and foundation
grants with a 100% on-time reporting rate and no material findings from any programmatic audit.

Our leadership team brings deep subject-matter expertise and a track record with the funder
priorities outlined in this NOFO. Executive Director Jane Holloway (MSW, 18 years) previously
served as regional director at Children's Defense Fund. Program Director Marcus Webb (MPH)
designed the evidence-based trauma-informed care model now used across four partner sites in
Midland County.

MYS maintains a full-time grants management staff of two, with dedicated systems for financial
reporting (QuickBooks Nonprofit), evaluation (REDCap), and compliance tracking (GrantTracker Pro).
Our most recent A-133 audit (FY2024) identified zero findings. We carry general liability, D&O,
and professional liability coverage appropriate to our operating budget of $3.1M.

Key infrastructure strengths relevant to this award:
- Licensed clinical social workers on staff (8 FTE) — no subcontracted clinical capacity needed
- Secure EHR system (Theranest) in use since 2019, HIPAA-compliant
- Established data-sharing agreements with Midland County Schools and Midland Health System
- Three existing program sites within the target service area, each ADA-compliant

In the past five years, MYS has successfully managed concurrent federal awards from SAMHSA,
the Office of Juvenile Justice (OJJDP), and the U.S. Department of Education — all in good standing.
