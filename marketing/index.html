What it does

/grants-scan — your morning shortlist

Pulls every posted and forecasted opportunity from grants.gov, scores each one against your keywords, preferred agencies, ALNs, and award-size window, then surfaces the top 15 with a one-line "why this fits" and the deadline math already done.

/grants-draft — RFP to skeleton in 30 seconds

Point it at an RFP URL or PDF. Get back a Markdown outline with every required section as an H2, the funder's exact heading text, word/page limits pulled verbatim, the evaluation rubric, and the attachments checklist. You bring the substance.

/grants-boilerplate — past wins, not paste-jobs

Drop your past proposals into a folder. Ask for "org capacity for HRSA" — get the three most relevant past versions, rewritten in the funder's tone. Every reuse is logged so you get a warning before sending the same paragraph to the same funder twice in 18 months.

Why a Claude Code plugin instead of a SaaS

  • Your past proposals never leave your machine. No SOC 2 questions from your nonprofit clients.
  • No per-seat tax. Your team uses Claude Code already; this just sits on top.
  • Hackable. Profile is one JSON file. Add a state portal, swap the ranker, or pipe results into your own CRM. The whole thing is ~300 lines of Python.

What it's not

  • Not Instrumentl. No private foundation data — grants.gov only in v1, with state portals and Candid integration on the roadmap.
  • Not a writer. The skeletons and rewrites are scaffolds; you bring the substance and the judgment. That's the whole point.
  • Not a CRM. It dumps results to a Notion database if you want; otherwise it's a Markdown shop.
