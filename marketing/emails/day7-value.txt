Subject: One thing most grant writers miss in grants.gov

Hi {FIRST_NAME},

Quick tip from week one:

The grants.gov search2 API returns `forecasted` opportunities — not just `posted` ones.
Most writers don't see these because the website buries them.

Your /grants-scan already pulls them (the profile.json default includes `"forecasted|posted"`).
Forecasted ops are worth tracking because:
- You have months of lead time instead of weeks
- The funder is often open to pre-submission contact at this stage
- You can start a draft skeleton now before the competition

Try this in your profile.json:
   "max_days_until_deadline": 365

That'll surface forecasted ops with a close date up to a year out.
Re-run /grants-scan and look for `"status": "forecasted"` in the results.
