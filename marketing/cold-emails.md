# Cold email templates — GPA directory + state nonprofit associations

> **Targeting**: GPA member directory at https://grantprofessionals.org → filter to solo consultants and small firms in your state(s) of focus. State nonprofit associations also publish member lists; cross-reference for warmer outreach.
>
> **Volume**: send 10/day for 5 days, not 50 in one batch. You want to talk to the people who reply, not blast a list.
>
> **Personalization rule**: every email must reference one specific thing from their site or LinkedIn. If you can't find one, don't send.

---

>

> Install: [LINK]
>
> If it works, I'd value 15 minutes hearing what's broken or missing. If it doesn't, no pitch — just hit delete.
>
> [Your name]
> [Your role / firm if any]

Why it works: short, specific to their niche, no asks beyond reading. The "trying on 10 writers this week" frames you as exclusive without being precious.

## Reply playbook

When they reply "looks interesting, what's the catch":
> No catch. Scanner stays free. The paid pieces are the boilerplate vault (the reuse-log feature is the actual moat — funders notice copy-paste) and the RFP-to-skeleton drafter. Want a 15-min walkthrough this week or next?

When they reply "we use Instrumentl already":
> Got it. Instrumentl's strength is the foundation data — I don't compete there. Where I might still help: the local boilerplate vault (your proposals never go to anyone's cloud) and the RFP-to-skeleton drafter, which Instrumentl doesn't do. Worth 15 minutes?

When they reply "what about [state portal]":
> Not in v1 — federal-only via grants.gov. Which state(s) are blocking the most for you? If 3+ writers say the same one I'll prioritize it.

When they go silent after install:
> One follow-up at +5 days: "Did the scan run? If it errored, the most common cause is [X]." That's it. No second follow-up.
